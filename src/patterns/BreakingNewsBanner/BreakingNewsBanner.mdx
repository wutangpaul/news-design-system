# BreakingNewsBanner

An urgent, attention-grabbing full-width banner for a single breaking story — typically pinned
to the top of the page above the global header or directly beneath it. It composes `Badge` for
the leading label and `Link` for the headline, rather than reimplementing either.

## Why `aria-live="polite"`, not `role="alert"`

This banner uses `role="status"` with `aria-live="polite"` rather than `role="alert"`
(`aria-live="assertive"`). Assertive live regions are meant for feedback the user's own action
just triggered and needs immediately — think inline form validation appearing right after
someone submits. A breaking-news banner is the opposite: it can appear at any moment while the
reader is in the middle of doing something else entirely (reading an unrelated article,
filling out a different form). Interrupting that with an assertive announcement would cut off
whatever the screen reader was already saying, which is a jarring, un-asked-for interruption
for genuinely passive, non-urgent-to-the-user's-current-task content. Polite announcements queue
up and are read once the current speech finishes, which respects the reader's own activity while
still surfacing the update.

## Dismissible by design

The banner is always dismissible via a real `<button>` (keyboard-operable, with a default
accessible name of "Dismiss breaking news banner"). There's no configuration to make it
non-dismissible: breaking-news banners are inherently transient — once a reader has seen it,
forcing it to stay on screen only adds persistent clutter with no ongoing benefit, which runs
counter to giving the reader control over interruptive content.

Because the dismissed state lives inside the component, mount a fresh banner (e.g. set
`key={href}` on it from the parent) whenever a genuinely new story should replace the current
one. As a safety net, the component also resets its own dismissed state whenever the `headline`
or `href` prop changes on an already-mounted instance, so a prop update alone is enough to bring
a truly new story back on screen even without a remount.

## Do's and don'ts

- Do keep one banner per genuinely distinct breaking story — don't reuse the same mounted
  instance to represent two unrelated stories without changing `headline`/`href`.
- Do pass a real destination `href`; the headline is a navigational link, not a decorative
  label.
- Don't nest this banner's `Link` inside another link or button — like `Tag`, it renders its
  own interactive controls (the headline link and the dismiss button) as independent, focusable
  siblings.

## Accessibility

- `role="status"` + `aria-live="polite"` announces newly-appearing banners without stealing
  focus or interrupting whatever the screen reader is already reading (see reasoning above).
- The dismiss control is a real `<button>` with a descriptive `aria-label`, fully keyboard
  operable (focusable, activates on Enter/Space).
- The headline link keeps sufficient contrast against the banner's solid background in both its
  unvisited and visited states — visited-link dimming (a `Link` default, useful in body copy) is
  overridden here so a previously-read breaking story never becomes hard to read.
